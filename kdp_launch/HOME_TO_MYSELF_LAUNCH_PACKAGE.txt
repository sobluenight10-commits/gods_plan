PART A — BUSINESS ANALYSIS (SELLABILITY & UPGRADE LEVERS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STRENGTHS (WHY THIS CAN SELL)
1) Series flywheel: 12 covers / 12 “season episodes” creates repeat purchase and
   collectible completion bias — stronger than a one-off undated planner.
2) Clear avatar: stressed working adults, especially women 28–42, burnout,
   self-reclamation language maps to a proven journal/planner market.
3) Premium visual system: solid color + central foil-style icon reads “giftable”
   on thumbnails; series consistency builds brand recognition.
4) Structure hook: “great quote -> what I found -> how I implement” is teachable
   in the listing and reduces “blank page anxiety.”

RISKS (MITIGATE IN LISTING + PRODUCT)
1) Quote-heavy interiors: avoid long copyrighted excerpts; prefer short quotes
   with attribution where required, public domain, or original paraphrase.
   Position the book as a guided reflection system, not a quote anthology.
2) Undated planner competition is fierce: you must differentiate on EMOTION
   (reclamation, fortress, sanctuary) + SERIES STORY + interior elegance.
3) Print cost vs price: 6x9 / 8.5x11 page count drives minimum price; model
   royalty after KDP printing fee before final retail.

UPGRADES THAT MOVE THE NEEDLE
Product
- Volume-specific “theme page” (1 page): season thesis + 3–5 journaling prompts
  unique to that volume (reduces “same interior, different cover” feeling).
- One “signature ritual” per volume (e.g., Vol.6: 2-minute boundary script).
- Interior: registration marks + OCR hint page = differentiated “serious tool.”

Brand
- Author central + series page + consistent subtitle pattern.
- Lead magnet: printable “Week 0” one-pager (email list) — optional.

Amazon SEO
- Master keyword cluster: reclamation journal, burnout journal, boundaries
  journal, wellness planner undated, self-priority journal, sacred journal.
- Split tests: subtitle emphasizes OUTCOME (reclaim yourself) vs FORMAT (planner).
